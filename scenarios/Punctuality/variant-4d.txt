Thomas has a scheduled meeting with his team and arrives noticeably late. As he slips into a seat, he sees that the discussion is already well underway. Several points have been covered and he struggles to catch up. He notices a few colleagues exchanging glances but saying nothing. The discomfort settles in. He realizes his delay not only disrupted his own understanding but may have distracted others as well. Feeling guilty for the lack of preparation and the impression it gave, Thomas quietly resolves not to be late again for the sake of both respect and effectiveness.
